MathPad Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

MathPad is a free, browser-based algebraic equation solver. It runs entirely in your web browser — there is no MathPad server, and the developer does not operate any backend that receives or stores your data. This policy explains what happens when you choose to enable the optional Google Drive sync feature.

What MathPad accesses

MathPad never accesses any Google data unless you explicitly sign in with Google from within the app. If you do, it requests only:

  • Your email address (email sign-in scope) — used to show which account you are signed in to and to label saved versions of your file.
  • Per-file Google Drive access (drive.file scope) — this lets MathPad create and read only the files it makes itself (a MathPad folder containing your MathPad.json records, plus any additional files you create through the app). MathPad cannot see, read, or modify any other file in your Google Drive.

How your information is used

  • Your MathPad records (equations, variables, and settings) are saved to a file in your own Google Drive so they sync across your devices.
  • Your email address is shown in the app's interface and stored on your device so the app remembers you are signed in.
  • That is the entire purpose. MathPad does not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any unrelated feature.

Where your data is stored

  • In your browser — your records and sign-in token are kept in your browser's local storage so the app works offline and remembers your session.
  • In your Google Drive — the synced copy of your records lives in your own Drive account, under your control.
  • Nowhere else. MathPad has no server and no database. Your data travels only between your browser and Google's services; the developer never receives a copy of it.

What MathPad does not do

  • It does not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone.
  • It does not include advertising or third-party trackers.
  • It does not transmit your records or Google information to the developer or any third party.

Limited use of Google user data

MathPad's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Google user data is used only to provide the sync feature described above, and is not transferred to others except as necessary to provide that feature, to comply with applicable law, or with your consent.

Retaining and deleting your data

You are always in control of your data:

  • Delete the synced file in your Google Drive to remove the cloud copy.
  • Sign out from within MathPad to revoke the app's access to your Google account.
  • You can also remove MathPad's access entirely at any time from your Google Account permissions page.
  • Clear your browser's site data for MathPad to remove the locally stored copy.
